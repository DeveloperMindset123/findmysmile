The crossbite dental procedures described in the provided data involve the use of different techniques for correction. In Case 2, tongue blade therapy was utilized where the patient was instructed to rotate the oral part up and forwards using a tongue blade as a fulcrum. This method was successful in correcting the crossbite within a few weeks. In Case 3, Hawley’s with z spring appliance was employed to achieve the correction. The Z spring was activated by opening the coils with universal pliers to apply a specific force for a few weeks, leading to the desired outcome. In Case 4, a combination of Hawley’s with z spring appliance and 2/4 appliance was used for crossbite correction and alignment of the teeth in the arch. These procedures were effective in achieving the desired results without significant complications, emphasizing the importance of regular monitoring and follow-up appointments for maintaining proper bite alignment.
